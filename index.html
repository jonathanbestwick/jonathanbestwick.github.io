About Me

I love the Lord Jesus and am wonderfully indebted to His grace, His plan for my life and to praise Him in all I do, however menial. I thank God for the incredible blessing of my wife and boys and for the way he has directed my life. I am passionate about being a catalyst in the journey of young people fulfilling their potential and becoming more than they ever thought they could be. Add the outdoors and the incredible tools of experiential learning and residential or expedition camping to the mix and I am in my element. I am amazed by the human spirit and never surprised by human frailties including my own. I love unique people and enjoy the challenge of renewing messy or broken individuals. I am easily inspired by experienced people in the Christian Camping industry and love to learn from them even at the expense of my own pride. I tend to see the best in people and this is both a strength and a weakness. I don’t take myself too seriously but am completely professional when needed.

Executive Volunteer Involvement

Elected board member to represent the KZN province. CCSA is an association of Christian camps, ministries and individuals who all share a passion for venue and/or programme based ministry. The executive committee oversees all countries in the SADC region including Zimbabwe, Namibia and Mozambique. The committee manages the business of the association and seeks to support its members by keeping abreast of all local and international developments in the industry. Personal, individual contact and relationship building is key to supporting the membership. CCSA as an association is 43 years old. CCSA is accountable to Christian Camping International.

My role as chairperson of SU South Coast was to lead the regional executive committee as we supported the team of employed and volunteer staff ministering with the SU South Coast region. I provided direct support in an advisory and mentorship role to the Regional Director and established an environment where SU staff felt supported and fulfilled in their ministry. I stood down from the role in order to focus on my role as Chairperson at CCSA.

Leading a dynamic and opinionated team of Godly, strong willed leaders is a wonderfully rewarding challenge. It is a privilege to serve our Christian Camping industry and to have the opportunity to travel to other African countries to represent CCSA and to assist with association building and training. Leading CCSA through these corona affected times hasn the importance and impact of personal relationship building within our members as we have sought to be there for sites that have had to close and members who have lost their jobs over this difficult time.

CSCBG was created to provide “buy in” power to Christian Camp sites and has a structure of rebates that directly financially support CCSA. Wonderfully lead and run b y Werner du Plessis, my role as board member is to keep the accountability through all transactions, to support Werner in vision, encouragement and decision making and to help ensure future growth of this developing initiative.

Lead and managed by Chris Eksteen this company was set up to raise funds for Christian camps and environmental awareness programming. Its specific focus was to ethically approach, pitch, propose and tender to mega funders such as national Lotteries to fund and sponsor camping programmes. My role as board member was to ensure accountability and to support Chris Eksteen directly in an advisory capacity.

My role as Christian Camping International coach to West Africa is to develop and support new CCI association formations in Nigeria, Sierra Leone and Ghana. Together with Ross Bay of CCI New Zealand and Dr Muhia Karianjahi from Honeyrock USA we travel to West Africa holding conferences and trainings to develop and nurture forming CCI association committees and memberships.

Employment

Skogheim Christian Trust

Skogheim Christian conference centre is a large camp and conferencing centre situated on the South Coast of South Africa. Registered as an NPO and Public Benefit Organisation, Skogheim exists to serve those who serve in God’s kingdom. My current role as centre manager is part of a three person management team who manage the daily running of the centre as a whole. We are also responsible for the strategic direction and vision of Skogheim as a ministry. All managers share roles with my focus being on programming, ministry development, front of house systems, activity development, guest liaison and housekeeping management. I am the health and safety representative and responsible for all health and safety at Skogheim including the development and implementation of Covid-19 protocols and systems.

As well as continuing with my role as programme manager my responsibilities as operations manager included marketing, social media and IT responsibilities as well as the development and updating of Skogheim’s policies and protocols to an international standard. Ministry development, guest liaison and programming remained an important aspect of my role at Skogheim. My role also included the training and development of established staff.

I was originally employed to start and develop youth directed ministries at Skogheim. Impilo Neqiniso has been the primary ministry project that involved the designing of applicable and impactful programmes and the training of local facilitators to run specialized experiential learning camps for learners who can’t usually afford to go on “camp”. This required the raising of funds to cover the costs of such camps. I also oversaw the creation and running of the Daniel Generation Academy – An academy that funds and runs the tertiary development of deserving yet economically unfortunate students. This was an ongoing curriculum that lasts throughout the student’s tertiary education. It has been an incredible opportunity to design, run and facilitate unique programmes for specialised groups such as rehab centres, places of safety, special needs schools and provincial sports teams.

Outward Bound Australia

This is a non-profit organisation, aspiring to develop and help Australians reach their untapped inner-potential. A wide range of programmes are run including courses for broken families and youth at risk. Most courses are run as wilderness expeditions and the group carries all gear and equipment.

As Team coordinator for Western Australia my position encompassed many roles. I worked primarily in Walpole in the South West. There I was in charge of coordinating all behind the scene logistics that come with running expedition based courses for up to 8 groups (140 participants) at a time. This included developing programmes, food ordering, gear and equipment management and also base camp and vehicle maintenance. Instructors lived and worked in this remote area for up to five months at a time. Managing and maintaining a positive work, as well as living environment for these instructors was also a large part of my job. I also maintained and nurtured relationships with the local parks associations and landowners. I spent time training staff and further developing and expanding the area. My responsibility as Team Coordinator was also to handle and coordinate any major incidents such as bushfires or medical evacuations. During the winter months of the year I worked in Eastern Australia developing and trialing new expedition areas. I occasionally instructed high profile courses where a high level of facilitation and experience was necessary

Outward Bound South Africa

A Non Profit NGO that runs expedition based programmes around South Africa. Their primary objective is to help South African’s realize their limitless potential through powerful group facilitation techniques and using the outdoors as an experiential learning tool.

My role here was to lead outdoor personal development courses for extended periods of up to 21 days, focusing on adjudicated and disadvantage youths as well as school and corporate groups. The youth at risk were part of the Chrysalis developmental programmes in Cape Town. Many of these youths were recently released from juvenile prison and were particularly interesting to handle and develop. Whilst 21day expeditions alone with these types of individuals were particularly challenging it was also the most powerfully rewarding experience of my young life. The principles that Outward Bound was built on and the ability for the wilderness programmes to significantly improve the attitudes and aspirations of these individuals was awe inspiring.
